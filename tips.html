How to Apply Perfume

Apply to Pulse Points

For a longer-lasting scent, apply to areas where your veins are close to the skin — the wrists, neck, behind the ears, and inside the elbows.

Don't Rub, Just Dab

Resist the urge to rub your wrists together. Rubbing breaks down fragrance molecules and can alter the scent. Gently dab or let it air dry.

Moisturize First

Perfume lasts longer on hydrated skin. Apply an unscented lotion before you spritz to give the fragrance something to cling to.

How to Store Your Fragrance

Keep it Cool and Dark

Heat, light and humidity break down perfume. Store fragrances in a cool, dark, dry place — like a drawer — away from direct sunlight.

Avoid Shaking the Bottle

Shaking introduces air bubbles which can accelerate oxidation and spoil the fragrance over time. Handle your bottles gently.

Keep the Original Box

The original packaging is designed to protect the bottle from light, providing an extra layer of protection for your investment.
